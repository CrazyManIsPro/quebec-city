Welcome to Quebec City

Discover the beauty of Quebec City, rich in culture, history, and great food.

Places to Visit

Montmorency Falls

Activities: Hiking, suspension bridge, zip line, ice climbing in winter.

Dining: Restaurant with waterfall views.

History: 30 meters higher than Niagara Falls!

❮
❯

Old Quebec (Vieux-Québec)

Activities: Walk through cobblestone streets, visit boutiques, enjoy local street art and performers.

Food: Local bakeries and French bistros like Le Cochon Dingue.

History: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to 400 years of French heritage.

Plains of Abraham

Activities: Biking, skiing (winter), historical reenactments.

Food: On-site food trucks and picnic-friendly areas.

History: Key battlefield in 1759 during the Seven Years' War.

Observatoire de la Capitale

Activities: 360-degree panoramic views of Quebec City, interactive exhibits.

Food: Nearby cafes and coffee shops like Café Krieghoff.

History: Located at the top of the Marie-Guyart Building.

Activities

  • Exploring Old Quebec’s historic charm
  • Attending the Winter Carnival

History of Quebec City

Founded in 1608 by Samuel de Champlain, Quebec City is one of the oldest European settlements in North America. Its historic core, Old Quebec, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring fortified city walls, colonial architecture, and centuries of cultural evolution.
